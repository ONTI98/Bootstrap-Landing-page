RABOSULA TREKKING

In partnership with the GWM P300 series
Rocketmods Travels presents the Rabosula trekking experience.
where technology and nature collide.

What to expect

Before heading to Rabosula, our drivers will take you through the popular heritage site, Tswaing crater.
The historical site-seeing experience adds on to the Rabosula trekking experience.

Meet the Rabosula locals

The locals are known for their resilience.
They have, by the president of South Africa,been deemed,
"The most hardworking individuals in this land."
The friendly locals will gladly take you through their daily life,
give you a glimpse of rural South Africa and a hands-on experience of their booming argricultural life.

Yellow garden spider
A common spider in Rabosula. The larger female has a yellow and black scalloped abdomen. These spiders are harmless to man.

Chicken coop
Poultry is a vital source of food in Rabosula. These coops are generally created of a blend of wire mesh and galavanized zinc sheets. They keep chickens safe from predators.

A hand-made chair
Craftsmen make chairs from steel and custom cushions. The fabrics are sources from a local factory founded and to this day, still ran by the locals.

Farming
This is the field that keeps Rabosula alive. Mr Manyeneng shows his son a fungus forming in one of his crops. This curiosity sparks intelectual dialog between the two.

Discovery
The conversation continues. Mr Manyeneng suggests looking at other crops. They discover there might be more infested crops. Action needs to be taken.

Research
The fungus has been named.Mr Manyeneng makes a hard but vital decision of burning any remaining crop as a prevention measure. No more loss will be accepted.

Exotic flora
Rabosula is teaming with exotic Monopi litchie trees . They were discovered in 1901 by the ancient people of the Osarejalo tribe in their quest to find dietry sweeteners to suplement their locally made honey.

Time-off
One of the drivers poses before sunset. The drivers shares stories he has heard about Rabosula.

Corn Smut
Mr Manyeneng's son shows us the Corn Smut fungus. Although in other parts of the world,Corn Smut fungus is treated as a delicacy, In Rabosula, they locals are weary of it.

Brickmaking
In Rabosula, houses are built with hand-made bricks from locally sourced cement and sand. The locals show us their bricks in their final stages the curing process.

GWM P300
The P300 soaks the sun in as it gears for yet another trip. This workhorse paired with our great drivers is undeniably made for the rabosula terrain.

Poultry mascot
Meet Runt, the local mascot. She is reported to have escaped so many slaughtering incidents that the locals decided to keep her as a permanent egg-layer.
